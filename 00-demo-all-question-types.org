** Question 11
Explain in 2-3 sentences why K-Means can converge to different solutions depending on initial centroid placement.

Answer:
K-Means uses an iterative optimization that finds a local minimum of
WCSS, not the global minimum. Different initial centroid positions
create different Voronoi partitions in the first step, leading the
algorithm down different optimization paths. Running K-Means multiple
times with different seeds (as ~n_init~ does in scikit-learn) mitigates
this by selecting the best result.
